About Us


Ole Kirk Christiansen

TLEGO's story begins at a Danish lumber mill in the 1910s. At that time, Billund was still a little-known town and Ole Kirk Christiansen (1891 - 1958) was just an ordinary carpenter with many ambitious. When he was young, Christiansen had a hobby of chiseling and carving wood, so he decided to open a carpentry workshop in 1916.





Jorgen Vig Knudstorp

During a time of crisis, Jorgen Vig Knudstorp joined Lego as a consultant when he was only 33 years old. He told Channel NewsAsia: "Lego almost lost itself. The brand is not as strong as it used to be, no one knows what the vision and mission of the company really is."

The name Lego comes from "Leg godt"

Feeling that it was extremely difficult to find enough wood for furniture during the Danish Depression of the 1930s, Ole Kirk Kristiansen began turning small pieces of wood into children's toys. Some of LEGO's early products include ships, cars, and a wheeled wooden duck that squawked when pulled back.
